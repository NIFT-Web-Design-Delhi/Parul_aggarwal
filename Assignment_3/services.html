OUR ARTISANS

Our focus group of artisans has been the Bhat family who has been practicing the craft of Kathputli for seven generations. For a family like theirs, Kathputli is their passion and their bread & butter. They have never known anything beyond the craft to pursue as a career. We, the team of Kaasth Katha had the privilege to speak with Vinod and Arjun Bhat of the family. In our fruitful discussion they explained to us the technical as well as creative process of the Kathputli craft, reminisced about their best experiences as an artisan group and opened up to us about their post-pandemic struggles. This website is a small initiative to get them the reach, coverage and appreciation they deserve.

The Puppet Show

In the puppet shows of Rajasthan there are a number of wooden dummy dolls that are dressed up in bright and color attires. These puppets are attached with strings that the puppeteer uses to move them around. A miniature stage is set up with the help of a cot that is laid by length. The cots are covered with sheets in order to conceal the puppeteer from the view of the public. Loud beats on the dholak (a kind of drum) announce the starting of the puppet show. Throughout the show, there is hooting and whistling by the puppeteers. The play is narrated by one of the puppeteers. The narration is accompanied with appropriate songs and beats on the drums that are in perfect harmony with the movements of the puppets. The shows, usually lasting one hour, are enjoyed by people belonging to all the age groups.
